## Why

Reviewer reports currently separate hard blockers from PM suggestions, but they
do not give PM and repair workers a consistent quality scale. This makes shallow
passes look equivalent to high-quality passes and leaves quantitative gaps such
as missing item counts or short deliverables too dependent on ad hoc prose.

## What Changes

- Add a strict Reviewer quality-score rubric using existing review result text
  fields, not a new runtime schema field.
- Define `6/10` as the minimum user standard just met, `9/10` as the target
  high-quality pass, and `10/10` as substantially exceeding the user's standard.
- Treat explicit quantitative requirements as hard gates when current and due:
  item counts, word counts, coverage rows, required ids, evidence counts, and
  similar bounded quantities must be compared as required/delivered/gap.
- Preserve PM authority: scores below `9/10` with hard gates met are PM
  decision-support, and PM always owns whether to optimize, continue, repair,
  waive, stop, or ask the user.
- Carry Reviewer score context into PM repair/optimization prompts and worker
  repair packets so repair work aims at the `9/10` target without giving
  Reviewer route or execution authority.
- Extend the fake-AI review-window profile matrix and focused tests to exercise
  scored passes, soft low-score suggestions, quantitative hard blockers, wrong
  soft-score blockers, and reviewer recheck score context.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities

None.

### Modified Capabilities

- `formal-gate-review-standards`: Reviewer packets and review guidance must
  include the high-standard score rubric, text-only score placement, blocker
  separation, and quantitative hard-gate rule.
- `role-scoped-quality-repair-prompts`: PM and worker repair prompts must carry
  Reviewer score context and make clear that PM always owns optimization
  decisions, even without a blocker.

## Impact

- Runtime kit role and phase cards for Reviewer, PM, and Worker.
- Current-scope repair packet instructions generated by the black-box runtime.
- Contract-driven fake-AI review-window profiles and coverage matrices.
- Focused prompt, runtime, contract-surface, and fake-AI replay tests.
