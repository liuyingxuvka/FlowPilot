## Why

Current FlowPilot already provides a strong single-path runtime, independent review, and FlowGuard-backed closure, but several prompts and models still encourage over-fragmented worker tasks, a mandatory special material-scan stage, and reports that do not expose whether an AI actually planned and completed a substantial body of work. As frontier AI becomes more autonomous, FlowPilot must differentiate itself through durable high-standard orchestration: every substantive AI role performs a complete bounded workstream, its plan and evidence remain auditable, and the existing independent review/FlowGuard chain can detect shallow or incomplete execution without creating a second runtime authority.

## What Changes

- Define one common complete-workstream lifecycle for every substantive AI role: understand, write a numbered plan, assess risk/FlowGuard need, execute and delegate, integrate, verify, self-repair, and submit.
- Require each substantive AI report to carry the role's numbered plan, per-step status, evidence, deviations, unresolved work, and final self-check inside the existing report/`Contract Self-Check` surface. This is a semantic quality contract reviewed by Reviewer, not a new Runtime hard gate or plan ledger.
- Reframe route leaves as the smallest independently accountable complete workstreams. PM still owns route, product, acceptance, ordering, and integration; Workers own a serious local execution/verification plan inside the accepted packet boundary.
- Strengthen PM's startup and ongoing prompt contract so a FlowPilot run is treated as a long, complex, high-standard project and every packet result is integrated into the whole project rather than merely marked locally complete.
- Keep formal FlowGuard at product/route/mutation/post-result/model-miss/parent/terminal boundaries, allow any substantive role to use role-local FlowGuard when process/state/coverage risk warrants it, and forbid self-approval.
- **BREAKING** Remove the mandatory special material-scan phase, dedicated material-sufficiency startup gate, and material-specific positive contract authority. PM reads available material normally; missing evidence or research becomes an ordinary existing PM role-work packet/batch, with optional Reviewer checks when risk warrants them.
- Preserve mandatory lightweight local capability/skill/environment inventory. Runtime owns mechanical discovery and freshness; PM selects relevance; only selected skills are deeply read and projected into role-scoped standards/evidence.
- Make the material artifact map an optional derived navigation index, never a mandatory form, allowlist, gate, or completion authority.
- Extend the existing canonical `ContractDrivenFakeAIResponder.from_open_packet_result` with semantic workstream profiles and use the same real packet/open/submit/review path for positive, shallow-plan, incomplete-plan, contradictory-evidence, stale-evidence, delegation-not-integrated, FlowGuard-misuse, and corrected-retry cases.
- Expand finite contract coverage with declared/applicable/excluded/generated/selected/executed/passed/failed/stale/proof-backed accounting, full finite static enumeration, every single mutation at its real owner, pairwise public-path coverage, named high-risk triples/selected four-way interactions, historical misses, fuzz, fake projects, and bounded live-AI samples. Claims remain limited to declared finite universes and never imply exhaustive arbitrary-language correctness.
- Reuse the current packet/result families, PM dispositions, Reviewer repair path, FlowGuard reports, MTA, TestMesh, ModelMesh, topology, background evidence, install sync, and version/release surfaces. No second route tree, constitution, checklist authority, report family, compatibility parser, or fallback path is introduced.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities

- `complete-ai-workstream-orchestration`: Common substantive-role lifecycle, role-local planning/evidence contract, PM integration duty, Reviewer audit, and FlowGuard self-approval boundary.
- `ordinary-resource-discovery`: Single-path local capability inventory plus ordinary PM material/research work packages without a mandatory special material stage.
- `finite-ai-contract-exhaustion`: Canonical fake-AI semantic profiles and honest finite Cartesian/interaction coverage accounting.

### Modified Capabilities

- `flowpilot-packet-review-flow`: Reviewer evaluates the submitted role plan and its execution evidence after PM absorption, while PM dispositions integrate results into the whole route.
- `role-scoped-quality-repair-prompts`: All substantive roles plan, verify, self-repair, and report complete bounded workstreams under their existing authority.
- `role-child-skill-use`: Local skill inventory remains mandatory but is independent of material intake; only PM-selected skills are deeply loaded and evidenced.
- `material-artifact-map`: The map becomes optional derived navigation evidence and no longer implies a mandatory material scan or sufficiency gate.
- `flowguard-test-obligation-ownership`: Role-local FlowGuard is risk-triggered and formal independent FlowGuard remains at named boundaries; no universal per-leaf pre-worker model is required.
- `synthetic-agent-coverage-matrix`: Coverage uses one canonical fake responder and explicit finite-universe accounting.
- `tiered-flowpilot-test-validation`: New workstream/material/skill regressions participate in focused, parent, background, release, and freshness gates.
- `flowguard-boundary-test-alignment`: Model obligations, source contracts, ordinary tests, and negative removals align directly for the changed workstream and material boundaries.

## Impact

The change affects FlowPilot role and phase cards, runtime-generated handoff guidance, current packet/result contract projections, route-decomposition wording, discovery ownership, material-specific router surfaces, FlowGuard capability/meta and focused child models, fake-AI profiles, Cartesian generators, MTA/TestMesh/ModelMesh registrations, tests, topology, SkillGuard evidence, README/CHANGELOG/version records, and the installed local FlowPilot copy. Current packet/result identities and the single Runtime/Router path are retained; removed material-specific surfaces receive negative tests rather than compatibility translation.
